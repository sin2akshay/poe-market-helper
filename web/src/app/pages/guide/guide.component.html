Making money with this data

Every strategy below is a real, community-proven way to earn currency in PoE2, tied to the dashboard section that supports it. Two things to know about the data first: values refresh from poe.ninja roughly hourly, and the "7-period change" sparklines are a fixed window from poe.ninja — while the history charts on the Currency and Unique items pages grow with every snapshot you ingest, so run npm run ingest regularly to make the longer time ranges meaningful.

1. Currency Exchange arbitrage low risk · steady

The in-game Currency Exchange is a real order book, which means real bid/ask gaps. Find a pair where the spread between buy and sell orders is 5–10%+, place a buy order slightly below market, resell slightly above, repeat and reinvest. Patient orders routinely fill 5–15% below market.

Use: the Flip finder's Currency churn table. High volume is the whole game — a wide margin on a dead market never fills. Movement in either direction is your trigger: a dip on a high-volume market is an entry, not a warning. Cross-check the Overview's Most volatile list — volatility on liquid markets is repeated entry/exit opportunities.

Risk: meta shifts. One popular build guide can repriced an orb overnight. Prefer many small flips over one big position, and check the sparkline direction before committing.

2. Multi-hop conversion chains medium effort · higher margins

Direct A→B flips get competed down fast. Chains (A→B→C→A) often retain 20–60% total margin because fewer traders bother with the math. Example from the community: buying catalysts at 7.2 per exalt and selling at 6 per exalt is ~20% per cycle — chaining through a third currency can push it further.

Use: the Currency page's "Best pair" column — it shows each currency's highest-volume trading pair and rate, which is exactly the graph you need to spot chain steps. The denomination toggle (top right) lets you sanity-check each leg in a common unit.

Risk: each hop adds fill time; rates can move mid-chain. Keep chains to 2–3 hops on high-volume pairs.

3. Liquid unique flipping medium risk · scalable

Buy popular uniques that are rising in demand (new build guide, patch buff, league meta settling) and resell into the rise. The key discipline: only flip items with plenty of listings — that's what guarantees you can exit.

Use: the Flip finder's Liquid item movers table (rising price, ≥30 listings) and the Overview's "Unique items heating up" list (value ≥ 0.5 div — meaningful money, real liquidity). Click any item on the Unique items page to see its price history across your snapshots.

Risk: you're competing with everyone reading the same trend. Enter early in the sparkline's rise, not after +500%.

4. Scarce snipes high risk · high margin

Items with very few listings and a spiking price: supply is drying up, and whoever holds the remaining copies sets the price. Buy the underpriced listings before others notice, relist high, wait.

Use: the Flip finder's Scarce snipes table. Treat these as position trades: money can sit for days, and with under ~10 listings the "market price" may be one person's opinion. Verify actual listings on the official trade site before buying anything.

Risk: illiquidity cuts both ways — the same thin market that made the spike can strand your relist. Never park money you need this week in a snipe.

5. Value-add crafting flips game knowledge pays

Buy cheap, add value, resell: quality up a popular unique, add a good corruption enchant, or gamble Vaal Orb rerolls on uniques whose corrupted outcomes sell far above the base (+1 socket, above-max rolls). The margin comes from knowing which outcomes the market pays for.

Use: the Unique items page to compare corrupted vs clean pricing within a category, and the Essences/Omens/Runes categories on the Currency page to time buying your crafting inputs when they dip.

Risk: Vaal outcomes are random — this is profitable in aggregate, not per item. Budget for bricks.

6. Farm what the market wants for players, not traders

Most currency is still made by playing — the edge is farming the mechanic whose loot is currently expensive. This league's community consensus: Expedition (with rune-slot encounters) has the biggest loot explosions, Abyss is the most consistent per-map earner, Ritual is the best zero-investment start (petition splinters), and Breach+Delirium gives the highest monster density for raw drops.

Use: the Overview's Category pulse table. A category with high total volume and positive average change (e.g. Ritual or Expedition trending up) means the market is paying for that mechanic's drops right now. Check it after each patch — farming yesterday's meta is the classic way to earn half rate.

Risk: everyone farms the meta, which eventually crashes the very prices that made it attractive. Rising volume + falling price = the crash is starting.

7. The divine hedge — league-cycle timing macro play

Exalts inflate over a league as everyone farms them; divines (and mirror-tier currency) hold value. Early league: sell leveling uniques and early drops at peak prices, convert savings to divines. Mid league: spend on your own gear while prices are sane. Late league: flip aggressively or wind down — item prices decay toward league end while hard currency holds.

Use: the Overview's "1 divine = X ex" card with its trend-since-first-snapshot, and the market breadth bars. Divine rising in exalted terms = your exalt savings are melting — convert. Broad item deflation with currency inflation is the classic mid/late-league signature.

Risk: none really — this is about not losing money passively. The only mistake is holding a big exalt pile for weeks.

8. Bulk premiums low effort side income

Bulk buyers pay a premium for one-stop stacks (maps, essences, splinters, soul cores). Aggregate small cheap stacks from many sellers, relist as bulk at 10–20% over per-unit price. Works because buyers value their time over the spread.

Use: the Currency page per category — fragments, essences, and splinter-type currencies with steady volume are the natural bulk candidates. The volume column tells you if there's enough flow to aggregate.

Risk: minimal, just slow capital turnover if you overpay while aggregating.

Reading the signals

  • Volume — the most important number for any flip. Real trade volume (currencies only) means you can enter and exit. No volume = no flip, whatever the margin looks like.
  • Listings — items don't have true volume data; listing count is the supply proxy. Many listings = liquid and price-trustworthy; few listings = scarce, risky, and possibly mispriced.
  • 7-period change + sparkline — poe.ninja's fixed trend window. Shape matters: steady climb = durable demand; single spike = probably one trade or a manipulation on a thin market.
  • Volatility (±%) — average swing size per period. On liquid markets, volatility is flip opportunity; on thin markets it's just noise.
  • Breadth — how much of the whole market is rising vs falling. Broad currency inflation + item deflation tells you to hold divines, not gear.
  • Category pulse — whole-mechanic trends. Farm rising categories, buy crafting inputs from falling ones.

Before you commit currency to any flip

  • Is the market liquid enough to exit? (volume for currencies, listings for items)
  • Is the trend from many trades or one? (sparkline shape, volume behind the move)
  • Did you verify the live price on the official trade site? This dashboard is aggregate data with up to an hour of lag.
  • Is your position size small enough that a meta shift won't hurt?
  • Are you early to the move, or buying someone else's exit?
